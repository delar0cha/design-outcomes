Linear posted a detailed breakdown of how they hire. Most companies don't write this down because writing it down forces honesty about what they actually value, and most companies haven't done that work.

Worth reading for design and product leaders for three reasons. First, the explicit tactics are reusable: paid work trials of two to five days, blind votes followed by group debrief, compensation discussed on the first call, slope weighted over credentials. None are revolutionary individually; the value is seeing them stitched together.

Second, the "fit" section is the kind of honesty most company hiring pages avoid. Linear says the absence of feedback loops energizes some people and disorients others, and that only one of those reactions describes someone who'll do their best work there. That's not a culture-fit dog whistle. It's a real filter.

Third, treating candidates with the same directness you treat the work is a tactic in itself.
