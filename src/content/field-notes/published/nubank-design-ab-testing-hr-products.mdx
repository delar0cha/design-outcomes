The finding worth pulling out isn't the decommissioning decision itself, but the gap it exposed: Recognition Index scores declined among recipients even as the tool's adoption climbed. Nubank ran an A/B test on an internal HR product with the same experimental structure they'd apply to a customer-facing feature, which is what made the gap visible at all rather than getting smoothed over by usage metrics. For teams evaluating internal tooling, the case for applying that same experimental discipline to people products is harder to make than to nod at, and this piece shows concretely what it looks like when it actually runs.
